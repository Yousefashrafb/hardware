Storage

Storage devices, such as hard disk drives (HDDs) and solid-state drives (SSDs), are used to store data permanently on the computer. They provide non-volatile memory, meaning data is retained even when the power is turned off.

Hard disk drives use spinning magnetic disks to store data, while solid-state drives use flash memory for faster read and write speeds. The choice between HDDs and SSDs depends on factors like performance, capacity, and cost.

Key factors to consider when choosing storage include capacity, speed, reliability, and form factor.
