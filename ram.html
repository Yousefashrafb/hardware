RAM - Random Access Memory

RAM, or random access memory, is a type of volatile memory that provides temporary storage for data and instructions that the CPU needs quick access to while the computer is running. It plays a crucial role in ensuring smooth and efficient operation of the system.

Modern RAM modules come in various form factors, including DDR3, DDR4, and DDR5, with capacities ranging from a few gigabytes to several terabytes. They offer high-speed data transfer rates and low latency, making them ideal for multitasking and memory-intensive applications.

Key factors to consider when choosing RAM include capacity, speed, and compatibility with the motherboard and CPU.
