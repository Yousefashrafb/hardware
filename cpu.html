CPU - Central Processing Unit

The CPU, also known as the central processor or microprocessor, is often referred to as the brain of the computer. It is responsible for executing instructions and performing calculations necessary for the operation of the computer system.

Modern CPUs come in various architectures and configurations, including single-core, dual-core, quad-core, and even multi-core processors. They feature high clock speeds and advanced instruction sets to handle a wide range of tasks efficiently.

Key factors to consider when choosing a CPU include clock speed, number of cores, cache size, and compatibility with the motherboard socket.
